Preconvention Institutes (PCIs)

Preconvention Institutes (PCIs) offer in-depth, hands-on professional development workshops designed and led by experts in the field of English language teaching and learning.

Topics are selected by the Professional Development, Research, and Convention Professional Councils in response to suggestions from convention planners and past PCI participants.

The TESOL ​2025 PCIs will be held on Tuesday, 18 March. Complete workshop information will be available in early November.

PCI 1  Teaching Pronunciation to Young Learners

Target Audience: All interested ESL/EFL teachers
Skill Level: Teachers with little or no background in teaching pronunciation.

This pre-convention institute focuses on current, innovative methods of teaching pronunciation to young learners. Based on research on pronunciation acquisition in early childhood, it offers a synthesis of age-appropriate strategies and practical approaches, emphasizing the importance of engaging, multi-modal methods and the use of technology in pronunciation teaching.

Presenters: Donna M. Brinton, Los Angeles, California, USA; Barry D. Griner, American Language Institute, University of Southern California, Los Angeles, California, USA; Brian Teaman, Osaka Jogakuin University, Osaka, Japan; Tamara Jones, Howard Community College, Columbia, MD; Di Liu, Temple University College of Education and Human Development, Philadelphia, PA

PCI 2  Integrating Social-Emotional Learning in Project-Based English Language Classrooms

Target Audience: Middle School Teachers to High School and University Professors
Skill Level: All levels of expertise

Explore the integration of Social-Emotional Learning (SEL) in project-based English language classrooms. This session highlights practical strategies to enhance students’  emotional intelligence, collaboration, and communication skills while engaging in meaningful projects, fostering a holistic approach to language learning and personal growth.

Presenters: Bamukunda Hillary, Uganda National English Language Teachers' Association Kampala, Uganda; Holly Marvin World Learning Organization, Arlington, Virginia, USA; Bismarck Agbemble, Liberty University, Richmond, Virginia

PCI 3  Culturally and Linguistically Responsive Special Education Processes for English Learners

Target Audience: K-12 educators
Skill Level: All levels of expertise

This workshop will support educators in understanding the basics of special education and distinguishing disability-related needs. The afternoon will focus on supporting dually-identified students through linguistically appropriate IEPs’.  Hands-on activities throughout the session will allow participants to engage with data, research, scenarios, and best practices.

Presenters: Jill Hakemian, Bank Street College of Education, Winter Park, Florida, USA

PCI 4  Be Exemplary: TESOL’s 6 Principles in the 21st Century Classroom

Target Audience: K-12 school teachers and Adult Educators
Skill Level: All levels of expertise

Weaved through TESOL’s 6 guiding Principles for Exemplary Teaching, “Be Exemplary” equips language educators with 20+ ready-to-use activities and strategies for integrating Social-Emotional Learning (SEL), gamification, community engagement, and Artificial Intelligence (AI), regardless of teaching context or content. Transform your classroom into a dynamic hub of 21st-century learning!

Presenters: Karamjeet Singh, English Directorate of Education, Government of Delhi, India; Aditi Bhasin, English Directorate of Education, Government of Delhi, India; Kamayani Joshi, English Directorate of Education, Government of Delhi India; Aanchal Arya, Shimla, India

PCI 5  Curriculum and assessment design in the age of AI

Target Audience:  Teachers, teacher trainers, curriculum designers and ELT managers  
Skill Level: Participants with little or no experience of using AI or those who want to expand their knowledge of ways to exploit AI in curriculum and assessment design

O’Sullivan (2021) argues that curriculum, delivery and assessment must be inextricably linked for any Comprehensive Learning System (CLS) to function. In this hands-on workshop, we will look at designing curricula which link teaching and assessment to the learning outcomes while exploiting AI using practical activities related to the participants’ contexts.

Presenters: Dr Carolyn Westbrook, SFHEA, Southampton, UK; Kevin Westbrook, FHEA, DipTransIoL, Southampton, UK

PCI 6 Dyslexia in the EFL Classroom

Target Audience: Teachers
Skill Level: Intermediate

This presentation explores effective strategies for teaching dyslexic students in the EFL classroom and highlights evidence-based practices, including structured literacy and multisensory approaches. It offers practical insights and techniques for educators to effectively support dyslexic students, fostering improved literacy skills and academic success in the EFL context.

Presenters:  Anna Rzepecka-Karwowska, University Integrated Language Method Academy & University of Windsor, Windsor Ontario, Canada; Sandra Walecka Polska Przystan Integrated Language Method Academy Fort Myers, Florida, USA

PCI 7  Techniques for Teaching Content Through English

Target Audience: Teachers of content through English and the use of English as Medium of Instruction
Skill Level: All levels of expertise

Teaching content through English (e.g., English as medium of instruction, CBI) in higher education offers opportunities, but risks abound. Participants learn ways to maximize benefits by designing “high structure” courses that maintain rigor while making effective use of class discussion and peer interaction to support language and content learning.

Presenters: Christopher Stillwell, Saddleback College/University of California, Irvine, California, USA

PCI 8  Enhancing group discussion skills through Tabletop Role-playing Games (TTRPGs)

Target Audience: Instructors at any level who would like to learn more about how to incorporate tabletop roleplaying into their language courses.
Skill Level: All levels of expertise

Tabletop role-playing games (TTRPGs) are effective tools for students who want to develop group discussion skills. In this session, the presenter will explain the basics of TTRPG play in the language classroom. Participants will develop characters, engage in group scenarios, and reflect on TTRPG applications for listening and speaking practice.

Presenters: Robb McCollum, Southern Utah University, Cedar City, Utah, USA

PCI 9 Inclusive ELT: Embracing Adaptive Learning, Digital Citizenship, and Intercultural Competence

Target Audience: ESOL educators, language teachers, curriculum developers, teacher educators
Skill Level: All levels of expertise. Participants should have:

  • Basic understanding of English Language Teaching (ELT) principles.
  • Experience in using digital tools in education.
  • Familiarity with digital literacy concepts.

This workshop equips secondary educators with strategies to integrate digital citizenship and intercultural competence into ELT using adaptive learning technologies and inclusive practices. Participants will engage in digital storytelling, cultural exchange reflections, and interactive classroom games to foster plurilingualism and global citizenship, effectively preparing students for an interconnected world.

Device Requirements:

Participants need to bring their own device, preferably a laptop. Tablets and smartphones may be used for some activities but laptops are recommended for a more seamless experience.

Presenters: Sonia Rocca, Lycée Français de New York, New York, New York, USA

PCI 10  Creating an Anti-bullying Environment through Social and Emotional Learning Instruction

Target Audience: School and Campus level teachers, teacher educators, and administrators.
Skill Level: Some foundational knowledge of bullying, and concepts of Social and Emotional Learning (SEL). Participants are expected to engage in discussion and problem-solving strategies.

This presentation proposes comprehensive activities in the English Language Classroom to engage students in defining, identifying, and responding to bullying. With discussion and presentation, the proposal aims to foster a deeper understanding of bullying through sample activities and encourage the participants to implement similar activities in their educational context.

Presenters: Bhola K.C., Triyuga Secondary School, Udayapur, Nepal

PCI 11 Creating a Successful Program to Address Newcomer and SIFE Learners

Target Audience: K-12 ESL teachers, mainstream elementary and secondary content teachers, supervisors, program directors, and administrators.
Skill Level: All levels of expertise

Newcomer programs are specially designed programs for new arrivals with little or no English, and, often, interrupted home language education. The participants learn how to implement five best practices for supporting new arrivals from program design to content development, building literacy, numeracy, and collaboratively building learner resilience, especially after trauma.

Presenters: Judith B. O’Loughlin,  Language Matters Education Consultants, Portland, Oregon, USA; Brenda Custodio,  Newcomer and ELLServices, Columbus, Ohio, USA

PCI 12  How to ENABLE teacher learning

Target Audience: Teacher educators, professional development experts, and mentors.
Skill Level: Beginner/Intermediate

In this practical workshop, participants will learn about the ENABLE framework for teacher education which seeks to provide principled mediated professional learning by incorporating the latest developments in research with classroom-based best practices. Participants will learn a new way to support the professional learning of colleagues throughout their career paths.

Presenters: Gabriel Díaz Maggioli, Institute of Education, Universidad ORT, Uruguay

PCI 13 Curriculum Development for ESL/EFL Professionals’ Designing to Meet Student Needs

Target Audience: ESOL Educators, Curriculum coordinators, Language program administrators from IEP, College/University, and Adult Education contexts
Skill Level: All levels of expertise

The present workshop provides a comprehensive organizational approach for novice and experienced curriculum designers to visualize all aspects of their curriculum so they can confidently take action on curricular weak spots. The presenters will use practical activities and provide individualized coaching on curricular issues attendees bring from their own programs.

Presenters: Grant Eckstein, Brigham Young University, Provo, Utah, USA; Kate Matthews, Curriculum Coaches, Provo, Utah, USA; Sydney Pedersen, Brigham Young University, Provo, Utah, USA

PCI 14  Creating Accessible Digital Learning Content for Everyday ESL Classroom Materials

Target Audience: ESL/EFL Instructors/Professors/Teacher Trainers
Skill Level: All levels of expertise

Ensuring accessibility in digital learning materials is essential. This session introduces language educators to making accessible digital content: how to create accessible Word documents, presentations, and multimedia. Through demonstrations and activities, attendees will learn common tools to meet accessibility standards. Join us to enhance your skills and support inclusive education.

Presenters: Miralynn Malupa; Samuel Reeder, Foreign Service Institute, Arlington, Virginia, USA
